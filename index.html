Work cycle of COE office
Stage I : Appointment letter

The examination system begins with a crucial first stage involving the submission of panels by all Heads of Departments (HODs). Each panel includes key roles such as the question paper setter, moderator, examiner, coordinator, scrutinizer, and reviewer, all of whom must be approved by the Board of Studies (BOS). Once the HODs submit these panels to the Controller of Examinations (COE) office, the information is entered into the COE system. This systematic entry allows for efficient organization and tracking of personnel involved in the examination process. Following this, the COE office generates appointment letters for each service provider, ensuring they are officially recognized and informed of their roles. These letters are then dispatched through the COE office's official email, facilitating clear communication and timely updates. This structured approach not only streamlines the preparation for examinations but also enhances accountability and coordination among all involved parties.

Stage II : Receiving question papers

The process of submitting question papers begins as both internal and external setters prepare their materials for examinations. These individuals are responsible for creating rigorous and relevant questions that align with the curriculum and learning objectives. To streamline the submission process, a dedicated email address has been established exclusively for this purpose. This ensures that all question papers are centralized in one location, facilitating easy access and organization. By directing submissions to a specific email ID, the institution can manage incoming documents more efficiently, minimizing the risk of errors or misplaced files. Additionally, this method enhances communication between question paper setters and the COE office, allowing for prompt feedback and any necessary revisions. Overall, this structured approach contributes to the integrity and quality of the examination process.

Stage III : Moderation

Moderation is a critical step in finalizing the examination question paper, involving collaboration between internal and external paper setters. This process is initiated only after both sets of questions have been submitted. The moderator, in conjunction with the coordinator, meticulously reviews the submitted questions to ensure they meet academic standards, align with learning objectives, and maintain balance in difficulty. During this review, adjustments may be made to enhance clarity and coherence, ultimately ensuring the quality of the assessment. Once the moderation process is complete, a finalized copy of the question paper, known as the manuscript, is prepared. This manuscript is then securely packaged in a sealed envelope to ensure confidentiality and integrity before being submitted to the Controller of Examinations (COE). This structured approach not only reinforces the reliability of the examination but also ensures that all parties involved uphold the highest academic standards.

Stage IV : Typing

The process of Typing starts after completing the Moderation process. Typing of question papers is done by the manuscripts. Most of the time the soft copy of the manuscripts is available, but in some cases typing is to be done by handwritten manuscripts. After completing the typing of question paper the paper has to be proof read several times to ensure error free question paper is available to the students in the examination hall. Co-ordinator is responsible for question paper proof check.

Stage V : Printing

Once the question paper proof check is complete, printing of question papers can be started according to the seating plan given by the different departmens. All the question papers must be ready by 1-2 days before the examinations in different envelopes room wise. For some departments the final printed question papers are checked by the OIC appointed by the departments. After the completion of checking process, the final printed question papers are dispatched to the departments on the day of exam or one day before the exams. Also the Hall tickets for Arrear candidates are also generated, printed and send to the departments.

Stage IV : Examinations

On the exam day, students take thieir seat according to the seat plan prepared by the departments. All the students are to be seated before 15 minutes of the exam. After the exam, all the answer scripts have to be counted and arranged according to the roll numbers and submit the same to the examination control room. There the scripts are to be packed and sealed along with the summary sheet pasted on each answer script packets. Then all the packets are send to the Controller Office with a consolidated dispatch sheet, which has to be checked with the packet summary sheet (top sheet).

Stage VI : Coding

Once the Answer scripts are submitted to the COE office, the answer scripts have to be unpacked and counted. Now the answer scripts coding starts. After the completion of answer scripts coding, the answer script Top-Sheet has been torn off and send for data entry and then the Answer scripts are packed in different colour packets as per the alloted department colour and the sticker labels (which contains packet information, the assigned examiners & Scrutinizers names with the barcode for scanning) are pasted on each packet.

Stage VII : Evaluation

After the examinations are conducted, the evaluation packets are prepared and ready within three days. Evaluation can commence only after the marking schemes for the subjects have been submitted, ensuring that examiners have clear guidelines for assessing student performance. Examiners can then collect their assigned packets from the front desk, but must first scan the respective packet to ensure accountability and tracking. This process facilitates efficient management and organization of the evaluation materials. Following the evaluation, the same meticulous procedure is applied to scrutinize the packets. This scrutiny step is crucial for verifying the accuracy of the evaluations and ensuring consistency in grading. By adhering to these structured protocols, the examination process maintains a high standard of reliability and fairness, ultimately contributing to the integrity of the assessment results.

Stage VIII : Data Entry

Once the answer script packets have been evaluated and scrutinized, the next step involves entering the respective award lists into the database. This crucial process ensures that all marks are accurately recorded and accounted for. After all award lists have been meticulously entered, the results are then processed. This processing involves compiling the data to generate final results for each student, ensuring that they reflect the evaluations conducted. The systematic entry and processing of these results are vital for maintaining the integrity and reliability of the examination outcomes. By utilizing a centralized database, the institution can efficiently manage student records, streamline communication, and facilitate timely announcements of results. This structured approach not only enhances the transparency of the assessment process but also reinforces the trust in the academic evaluation system.

Stage X : Review

After the publication of results, students are given the opportunity to review their answer papers. This process allows them to gain insights into their performance and address any concerns they may have regarding their evaluations. To initiate a review, students can conveniently apply online, simplifying the application process. They are required to pay a review fee, which covers administrative costs associated with the review procedure. This online application system not only enhances accessibility but also streamlines the review process, making it more efficient for both students and the administration. By offering this opportunity, the institution promotes transparency and encourages students to take an active role in their academic journey, fostering a sense of accountability and engagement in their learning experience.
