Work cycle of COE office
Stage I : Appointment letter

This is the first stage of Examination system. In this stage all the HOD's submit the panel (including Question paper setter, Moderator, Examiner, Co-ordinator, Scrutinizer, Reviewer) approved by BOS (Board of studies) to COE office. After entering all the panels in the system of COE office, COE office start generating appointment letters and send it to all the respective service providers through the official email of COE Office.

Stage II : Receiving question papers

The Question paper setters (Internal & External) start sending the question papers to the email ID dedicated only for question paper submissions.

Stage III : Moderation

Moderation is a process to finalize the question paper from Internal paper setter set and External paper setter set by the Moderator with the help of the co-ordinator. The process of Moderation starts only after the submission of question paper from Internal as well as External paper setter. After completing the Moderation, the finalized copy (Manuscript) is submitted to COE in a sealed envelope.

Stage IV : Typing

The process of Typing starts after completing the Moderation process. Typing of question papers is done by the manuscripts. Most of the time the soft copy of the manuscripts is available, but in some cases typing is to be done by handwritten manuscripts. After completing the typing of question paper the paper has to be proof read several times to ensure error free question paper is available to the students in the examination hall. Co-ordinator is responsible for question paper proof check.

Stage V : Printing

Once the question paper proof check is complete, printing of question papers can be started according to the seating plan given by the different departmens. All the question papers must be ready by 1-2 days before the examinations in different envelopes room wise. For some departments the final printed question papers are checked by the OIC appointed by the departments. After the completion of checking process, the final printed question papers are dispatched to the departments on the day of exam or one day before the exams. Also the Hall tickets for Arrear candidates are also generated, printed and send to the departments.

Stage IV : Examinations

On the exam day, students take thieir seat according to the seat plan prepared by the departments. All the students are to be seated before 15 minutes of the exam. After the exam, all the answer scripts have to be counted and arranged according to the roll numbers and submit the same to the examination control room. There the scripts are to be packed and sealed along with the summary sheet pasted on each answer script packets. Then all the packets are send to the Controller Office with a consolidated dispatch sheet, which has to be checked with the packet summary sheet (top sheet).

Stage VI : Coding

Once the Answer scripts are submitted to the COE office, the answer scripts have to be unpacked and counted. Now the answer scripts coding starts. After the completion of answer scripts coding, the answer script Top-Sheet has been torn off and send for data entry and then the Answer scripts are packed in different colour packets as per the alloted department colour and the sticker labels (which contains packet information, the assigned examiners & Scrutinizers names with the barcode for scanning) are pasted on each packet.

Stage VII : Evaluation

The packets are ready for evaluation after 3 days of conducting the examinations. Evaluation can start after the submission of the subjects marking scheme is received. The examiners can collect the their respective assigned packets for evaluation from the front-desk after scanning the respective packet. Post evaluation the same process is followed for scrutiny of the packets.

Stage VIII : Data Entry

Once the answer scripts packets evaluated and scrutinezed, the respective award lists are entered into the database. After entering all the award lists into the database, the results have to be processed.
